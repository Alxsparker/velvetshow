The key differences

  • One app vs two. ShowBuddy Setlist handles your tracks and lyrics. Show Buddy Active handles your lights. You need both — that's $318 minimum, two separate windows to manage live.
  • OSC output. Velvet Show sends OSC natively to MaestroDMX, QLab, Companion and any network-connected system. ShowBuddy Active can receive OSC (via TouchOSC) but doesn't send it.
  • MaestroDMX integration. Velvet Show connects directly to MaestroDMX — import your cue list, trigger scenes via MIDI or OSC, control Master Brightness. No equivalent in ShowBuddy.
  • iPhone remote. Velvet Remote is a free native iOS app that connects to Velvet Show over your local WiFi. ShowBuddy Active requires a third-party app like TouchOSC for wireless control.
  • Price. Velvet Show is $79 for everything. ShowBuddy Setlist + Active starts at $318.

What ShowBuddy does better

To be fair: Show Buddy Active has more advanced DMX capabilities — multiple universes, video output, a full fixture editor and a 2D visualizer. If you have a complex professional lighting rig and need deep DMX programming, Show Buddy Active is more powerful on the lighting side.

Velvet Show is the better choice if you want everything — backing tracks, lyrics, MIDI, OSC, lighting automation and iPhone remote — in one $79 app, without managing two separate pieces of software on stage.
